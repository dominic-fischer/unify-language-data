    Pluperfect:
      title: "Pluperfect"
      applies: {u-Aspect: [u-prf, u-hab, u-ipfv, u-iter, u-pfv], x-Temp-Relation: x-anterior}
      pattern: "haben/sein (preterite finite) + past participle"
      examples:
      - "u-prf: Ich hatte gearbeitet."
      - "u-pfv: Ich hatte das Buch gelesen."
      - "u-ipfv: Ich hatte lange gelesen, als er kam."
      - "u-hab: Ich hatte oft dort gegessen."
      - "u-iter: Ich hatte ihn mehrmals gesehen."
      note: "The pluperfect is used for anteriority, i.e. to describe events prior to another past reference point. It highlights resultative or perfect (u-prf), but also accommodates perfective (u-pfv), imperfective (u-ipfv), habitual (u-hab), and iterative (u-iter) readings in narratives."

      note: "The 'am'-progressive in the past is a colloquial form, highlighting an action in progress (u-prog). It can also serve as an imperfective marker (u-ipfv) for backgrounded ongoing activity. Rare in formal registers."

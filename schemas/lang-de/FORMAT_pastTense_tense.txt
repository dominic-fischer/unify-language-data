      note: "The preterite is the default narrative past in written German. It expresses habitual actions (u-hab), ongoing background situations (u-ipfv), repeated or iterative actions (u-iter), and completed bounded events (u-pfv). In spoken German, often replaced by the perfect."
      examples:

      - "u-iter: Er klopfte immer wieder."

      note: "The perfect is the preferred past form in spoken German. It encodes current relevance or resultative meaning (u-prf), but in usage also covers perfective (u-pfv), imperfective/background (u-ipfv), habitual (u-hab), and iterative (u-iter) readings depending on context."
      examples:
      - "u-prf: Ich habe gearbeitet."

    de-plusquamperfekt:
      applies: {u-Aspect: [u-prf, u-pfv, u-ipfv, u-hab, u-iter]}
      note: "The pluperfect is used for anteriority, i.e. to describe events prior to another past reference point. It highlights resultative or perfect (u-prf), but also accommodates perfective (u-pfv), imperfective (u-ipfv), habitual (u-hab), and iterative (u-iter) readings in narratives."
      examples:
      - "u-prf: Ich hatte gearbeitet."

    de-past-progressive:
      applies: {u-Aspect: [u-prog, u-ipfv]}
      note: "The 'am'-progressive in the past is a colloquial form, highlighting an action in progress (u-prog). It can also serve as an imperfective marker (u-ipfv) for backgrounded ongoing activity. Rare in formal registers."
      examples:
      - "u-prog: Ich war am Arbeiten."
      - "u-ipfv: Er war am Lesen."
